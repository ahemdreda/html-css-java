Manchester City Football Club, commonly known as Man City, is an English professional football club based in Manchester. The club was founded in 1880 as St. Mark's (West Gorton) and later became Ardwick Association Football Club before settling on its current name in 1894. Manchester City is one of the most successful football clubs in England, having won numerous domestic and international titles over the years. The club has won seven English Premier League titles, seven FA Cups, and seven League Cups, as well as three UEFA Cup Winners' Cups, one UEFA Cup, and one UEFA Champions League title. Manchester City plays its home matches at the Etihad Stadium, which has a seating capacity of over 55,000 spectators. The club has a fierce rivalry with Manchester United, with matches between the two clubs known as the Manchester Derby. The club's current manager is Pep Guardiola, who has led the team to much success since taking over in 2016. The team's current captain is Fernandinho, and the squad includes top players such as Kevin De Bruyne, Riyad Mahrez, and Raheem Sterling. In recent years, Manchester City has become known for its attractive and attacking style of football, as well as its commitment to developing young talent. The club has invested heavily in its youth academy and has produced several players who have gone on to play for the first team and for their respective national teams. Off the pitch, Manchester City is owned by the City Football Group, a global football organization that also owns other clubs such as New York City FC, Melbourne City FC, and Yokohama F. Marinos. The club is also known for its charitable work, with the Manchester City Foundation supporting a range of community initiatives across the city.

Manchester City has had a lot of recent success on both the domestic and international stage. Here are some of their major achievements in recent years: - Premier League titles: Manchester City has won the Premier League title seven times in total, with three of those titles coming in the last four seasons. They won the title most recently in the 2021-2022 season, finishing 12 points ahead of second-placed Liverpool. - Carabao Cup titles: Manchester City has won the Carabao Cup (formerly known as the EFL Cup) seven times in the last nine seasons. They won the cup most recently in the 2020-2021 season, beating Tottenham Hotspur 1-0 in the final. - FA Cup titles: Manchester City has won the FA Cup six times in its history, with the most recent victory coming in the 2018-2019 season. They beat Watford 6-0 in the final to complete a domestic treble (winning the Premier League, Carabao Cup, and FA Cup in the same season). - UEFA Champions League title: After years of trying, Manchester City finally won its first-ever UEFA Champions League title in the 2020-2021 season. They beat Paris Saint-Germain 4-1 on aggregate in the semi-finals before defeating Chelsea 1-0 in the final. - Domestic treble: In the 2018-2019 season, Manchester City became the first men's team in English football history to win the domestic treble (Premier League, Carabao Cup, and FA Cup) in a single season. Overall, Manchester City has established itself as one of the top football clubs in the world in recent years, with a talented squad and a track record of success both domestically and internationally.

Pep Guardiola is a Spanish football coach who is currently the manager of Manchester City Football Club.
